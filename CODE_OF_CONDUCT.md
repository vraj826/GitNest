# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors, maintainers, and community members of GitNest, pledge to create a welcoming, inclusive, respectful, and harassment-free environment for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual orientation.

Our goal is to build an open-source community focused on collaboration, learning, innovation, and mutual respect.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to a positive, healthy, and professional environment for all participants.

---

## Community Standards

### Positive Behaviour

Examples of behaviour that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Using respectful and inclusive language
- Being constructive during discussions and code reviews
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accepting feedback gracefully and learning from it
- Helping new contributors and encouraging collaboration
- Prioritizing the growth of the community over personal interests
- Showing empathy and professionalism in all interactions
- Maintaining a beginner-friendly and supportive atmosphere

---

### Unacceptable Behaviour

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct of any kind
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal attacks, intimidation, or threats
- Public or private harassment
- Sharing private information without consent
- Spam, malicious links, or disruptive behaviour
- Deliberate misinformation or harmful contributions
- Sexualized language, imagery, or inappropriate advances
- Demanding issue assignments without engagement, or repeatedly submitting low-effort/spam pull requests purely for program points
- Any conduct considered inappropriate in a professional environment

---

## Contributor Responsibilities

All contributors are expected to:

- Follow repository guidelines and contribution standards
- Write clean, maintainable, and respectful code/comments
- Respect maintainers' decisions during issue discussions and PR reviews
- Avoid unnecessary conflicts and disruptive arguments
- Properly test their contributions before submitting pull requests

Maintainers are responsible for ensuring fair moderation and fostering a healthy community environment.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers and repository administrators are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently.

They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, pull requests, issues, discussions, or other contributions that violate these guidelines, and may temporarily or permanently restrict any contributor for behaviour they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Moderation decisions will be made professionally and transparently whenever possible.

---

## Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, please report it through one of the following channels:

- **Email the project admin directly**: `ankitakk615@gmail.com`
- **Direct message a maintainer or mentor** on the project's Discord/community server
- For non-sensitive issues only, you may also use GitHub Discussions

All reports will be reviewed and handled confidentially, and only shared with individuals necessary to resolve the issue.

Retaliation against individuals who report violations in good faith will not be tolerated and is itself a violation of this Code of Conduct.


---

## Enforcement Actions

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining consequences for any violation of this Code of Conduct. The severity of the response will be proportional to the severity of the violation.


### 1. Warning

A private or public warning explaining the violation and the corrective behaviour expected going forward.

### 2. Temporary Restriction

Temporary restriction from discussions, pull requests, or community interactions for repeated or moderate violations.

### 3. Suspension

Temporary suspension from contributing to the repository or participating in project activities, for serious or repeated violations.

### 4. Permanent Ban

Permanent removal from the GitNest community for severe violations or a sustained pattern of unacceptable behaviour.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all GitNest community spaces, including:

- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, and Discussions
- Project documentation
- Community chat platforms (e.g. Discord)
- Social media or collaboration spaces associated with the project
- Events, hackathons, or contributor programs related to GitNest (including GSSoC)

It also applies when an individual is representing GitNest in public spaces, such as using an official project email address or posting via an official social media account.
